Welcome to the Maryland Department of the EnvironmentÆs (MDE) Responsible Personnel Training for Erosion and Sediment Control.  Maryland law defines ôresponsible personnelö as any foreman, superintendent, or project engineer who is in charge of on-site clearing and grading operations or sediment control associated with a construction project.  The law further specifies that responsible personnel on a construction project in Maryland must have a certificate of attendance at an MDE approved training program. 

 

Successful completion of this on-line video course, including passing of the final exam will provide the required certification.   The certification is valid for a minimum of three years.  However, it is automatically renewed unless you are notified that additional training is required.

 

The course is broken down into numerous three to seven minute videos with review questions available after each one.  It is recommended that the course be viewed in sequence and will likely require three to four hours to complete.  However, you may select any section at any time, including the taking of the final exam.  You may also log out and return as often as you like, in order to complete the course at your pace as well as review the material when needed.   

 

Links are also provided to the power point information used in the videos and to MarylandÆs  2011 Standards and Specifications for Erosion and Sediment Control, often referred to as the Handbook throughout the course.  Upon taking and passing the final exam, you will be able to view and print your certification card.
